HAMM/Mortar, about 5 billions unknowns on 40K cores using Feel++

01 septembre 2014

We are developing a substructuring preconditioner for the \(h\)-\(p\) mortar method. In order to corroborate the mathematical properties of this preconditioner we ran various test to check the dependency with respect to \(H\) (the number of subdomaines), \(h\) the grid size within the subdomains and \(p\) the polynomial degree used by the finite element approximation.

We have the following result

\\(\mathrm{cond}(P^{-1} A) \leq p^{3/2} (1+\mathrm{log}(\frac{H p^2}{h}))^2\\\)

where \(P\) is our preconditioner and \(A\) is the Schur complement associated to the \(h-p\) mortar discretisation of an elliptic problem.

We plot below the ratio for \(p=1,…,5\) and \(H/h = 80\)

\\(\lambda^p = \dfrac{\mathrm{cond}(P^{-1} A)}{(1+\mathrm{log}(\frac{H p^2}{h}))^2} \\)

as well as the number of iterations needed to decrease the relative residual by 1e-6

As you can see the number of iterations increase very little as \(p\) increases and stays constant or decreases as the number of subdomains increases which is of course a very nice feature.

Here is the table with the number of degrees of freedom corresponding to these calculations at 40000 cores, we reach about 5 billions of unknowns.

Order Unknowns
1 295’725’687
2 1’182’838’857
3 2’661’337’501
4 4’731’229’377

The last computation at \(p=4\) takes about 150s.

These computations have been done at the mesocentre of Strasbourg from 16 to 256 cores then on Curie at the TGCC in France up to 40000 cores !

This work is the result of the collaboration between Abdoulaye Samake and Christophe Prud’homme from Cemosis and Silvia Bertoluzza and Micol Pennacchio from IMATI.
